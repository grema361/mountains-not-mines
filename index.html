The global environment under accelerating stress

Land and ocean ecosystems have historically absorbed roughly half of human carbon emissions. But research published in Nature Climate Change shows terrestrial carbon uptake weakening under heat stress and drought.

Analysis by climate researcher Leon Simons indicates that Earth’s Energy Imbalance has more than tripled in just two decades.

Meanwhile the World Wildlife Fund reports an average 73 percent decline in monitored wildlife populations over the past 50 years.

A Guardian investigation also warns that modern food supply systems may be vulnerable to cascading failures triggered by climate shocks and infrastructure disruptions.
